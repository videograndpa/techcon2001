.

Mr. Lynn Claudy
Senior Vice President for Science and Technology, National Association of Broadcasters
Washington, DC

Lynn Claudy is Senior Vice President of Science and Technology for the National Association of Broadcasters in Washington DC.

As head of the Science and Technology department, he is responsible for representation of the NAB in all radio and television technical matters. He joined NAB in 1988 as a staff engineer and held positions of Director of Advanced Engineering and Technology and Vice President before assuming his present position in February 1995.

He has a Bachelor of Arts degree from Oberlin College, a Bachelor of Science degree in electrical engineering from Washington University in Saint Louis and a Master of Science degree in electrical engineering from the Illinois Institute of Technology. He is a member of Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, the Society of Motion Picture and Television Engineers, the Society of Broadcast Engineers and the Audio Engineering Society.
