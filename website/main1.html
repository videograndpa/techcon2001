The Public Broadcasting Service holds two Broadcast Engineering meetings each year; one in the fall of the year, usually in the eastern part of the country, and a spring Technology Conference, in Las Vegas, coordinated with the National Association of Broadcasters Convention.

This year, this spring meeting, PBS 2001 Technology Conference, will bring together almost 400 broadcast engineers and information technology professionals from PBS stations around the country, at the Alexis Park Resort Hotel in Las Vegas, for three days of discovery and discussion.
This site is designed to provide supporting materials for those who attend this year's Conference, as well as provide a virtual Conference experience for those who are not able to attend. It will exist in its present form (more or less) until April 17, 2001, when it will be revised to become the repository of all collected Conference materials, for download.

This information may change, so you will want to use the"refresh" button on your brower, on a regular basis, to insure you have the most recent page-version stored in cache.
